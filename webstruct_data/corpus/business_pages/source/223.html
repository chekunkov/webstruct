Your Smizer Mill Road Dentist

Taking the time to treat our clients right

Most dentistry is done in a reactive manner. For example, once a tooth has a cavity, it’s filled, crowned, or even removed. All of these things eliminate the cavity or problem, but none of them lower the risk for getting cavities.

Cavity risk is what allows the cavity to form and when risks are changed or eliminated, the cavity never forms. This goes way beyond brushing and flossing better! Unfortunately, this is never explained to most people.

At DeCo-Dental Family and Cosmetic Dentistry, risk assessment, education, and risk management are our priorities. This commitment has allowed us to help high risk individuals get stable, many for the first time in their life.

Everyone has heard of the four food groups and how important it is to be well balanced. At DeCo-Dental, your Smizer Mill Road dentist uses this concept to distinguish the four areas of the mouth that must be evaluated to develop a risk assessment, make a diagnosis and provide recommendations for our patients.

Periodontal - Gum and bone around our teeth
Biomechanical - Tooth structure (the above cavity example falls into this category)
Functional - Jaw joints, muscles, bite and chewing surfaces of the teeth
Dentofacial - Smile characteristics (esthetics)

It is proper diagnosis and management of these areas that allow us to minimize your risk in whichever category or categories you may have, and help you preserve your teeth and smile for a lifetime.

When you take the time to visit the friendly faces at DeCo-Dental Family and Cosmetic Dentistry, you can count on professional service with a friendly smile.

If you have any questions, our helpful team members are here to guide you. From the front office staff who are happy to help with insurance or billing concerns and work with you to ensure quick insurance payments and easy scheduling, to our highly trained dental hygienists and dental assistants, our team is committed to you.

We are dedicated to the ongoing oral health of each of our patients. Ask our staff members for a demonstration of proper brushing and flossing techniques, and we can give you some tips for the best ways to improve your smile and oral health.
